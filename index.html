Client Requirements
Create a website for Search Engine discovery and cataloging products and services. Essentially the website will be a Web Sales Channel.
Research & Analysis
Research
The research indicates the target demography for the website is in the age group of 25+ and is predominantly male.
Analysis
A competitor analysis was done by method of search engine queries. For the primary product category of Cement Products (manufacturer) the following competitors were identified:
  • Senpathi
  • The Manhole & Gully Shop (not cement)
  • PRYDA (not cement)
  • ICC & ELS (different category)
None of the competitors seem to directly compete with Astron products and services. This is an opportunity to reach the top of search rankings in a shorter period of time.
The competitors websites uses an approach where they highlight their product, services, and company qualities. None of the competitors have content offering the consumer knowledge about the product and its purpose. This is also an opportunity Astron can capitalize on, especially with the presentation skills of Mr. Dilshan Perera.
Proposed Solution
With low search engine competetion and the target consumer demography in mind, I believe using the website as a repository for information about the products and services (by having related keywords strategically structured) will help list the Astron website higher in search engine rankings. To achive this goal, the following is required;
  • Blog
  • Single page for each product
  • Linking blog posts to social media
  • Properly structured sitemap
  • FAQ
With enough content to initialise and if the content is periodically added/updated, each time search engine crawling bots visit Astron website for indexing, the changes will indicate the website is an active respository for information in your product/service category.
When potential consumers search for these keywords, Astron website will have a higher probability of being listed.
The FAQ (Frequently Asked Questions) section is cruicial. This is a page that will contain questions most commonaly search for that are related to Astron products/services. The FAQ should have brief answers with links to the relevent product/service.
This interaction of an users searching for a keyword, finding the FAQ, then visiting the linked product/service page will be identified as a pattern by the search engine algorythm. When this is iterated enough times, search engines will directly start linking searches to product/service pages.
